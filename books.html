Favorite Books

The Scourge of the Swastika

by Edward Russell

A powerful and grim account of the atrocities committed by the Nazis during World War II, based on the author's work as a prosecutor at the Nuremberg trials.

Why I Am An Atheist: An Autobiographical Discourse

by Bhagat Singh

A compelling essay by the Indian revolutionary, Bhagat Singh, outlining his philosophical and rational reasons for his atheism.

Shoe Dog

by Phil Knight

A candid and compelling memoir by the founder of Nike, chronicling the company's early struggles and its rise to global dominance. A true story about passion, perseverance, and the spirit of entrepreneurship.

Zero To One

by Peter Thiel

Offers a new perspective on innovation and business, arguing that true progress comes from creating new things ("zero to one"), not from copying others ("one to n"). An essential read for anyone interested in building the future.

Money Master the Game

by Tony Robbins

A comprehensive guide to financial freedom, providing a seven-step blueprint to master your finances and secure your future. It distills complex financial concepts into actionable advice for everyone.

Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind

by Yuval Noah Harari

A sweeping history of the human race, from the emergence of Homo sapiens in Africa to the radical and sometimes destructive revolutions of our modern era. This book challenges our core assumptions about what it means to be human.

Beyond Good and Evil

by Friedrich Nietzsche

Nietzsche's radical and provocative philosophical work that critiques traditional morality and philosophy, encouraging a "revaluation of all values." It explores the origins of moral prejudices and the nature of the "will to power."

Critique of Pure Reason

by Immanuel Kant

One of the most influential works in the history of philosophy, in which Kant seeks to determine the limits and scope of human knowledge and reason. A foundational text in metaphysics and epistemology.

by Friedrich Nietzsche

Bullshit Jobs: A Theory

by David Graeber

An anthropological critique of modern work, arguing that many jobs are pointless and contribute nothing to society, creating a sense of spiritual violence and unhappiness among workers.
